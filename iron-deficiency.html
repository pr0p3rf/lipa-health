The pattern we see every day

"My doctor said my iron was normal. So why am I exhausted?"

This is the most common dismissal pattern in primary care. Woman, 30s-40s, presents with fatigue, hair shedding, brain fog. Doctor runs CBC. CBC normal — hemoglobin 12.5, RBC 4.6. "You're not anemic. Maybe you're stressed. Try to sleep more."

Then the patient runs ferritin separately. Ferritin 18 ng/mL. The lab flags it green because the lab cutoff is 15. And no one tells her that 18 is not "normal" — it's symptomatic iron deficiency that progresses to anemia if left alone.

CBC is the last thing iron deficiency touches, not the first. Iron stores deplete first (ferritin falls). Iron utilization fails next (TSAT drops). Red cell production declines third (RDW rises, MCV drops). Hemoglobin falling to anemic range is the last stage. By the time CBC is "abnormal," the deficiency has been hurting you for months or years.

Lipa's iron deficiency interpretation
Textbook iron deficiency without anemia (IDWA). Your hemoglobin is technically normal at 12.4 — but RDW elevated at 15.2 is the early signal that red cell production is destabilizing. Ferritin 18 + TSAT 15% is functionally severe iron deficiency by every modern guideline (lab cutoff of 15 is decades out of date). Your hs-CRP of 0.8 confirms ferritin is genuinely low (not falsely low from inflammation). The probable contributors stack up: heavy periods (chronic blood loss), vegan diet (lower iron bioavailability), and B12 also depleting. Common questions.

My CBC is normal. Can I still be iron deficient?
Yes — common. CBC measures hemoglobin and red cell parameters, which only drop when iron deficiency has progressed to anemia. Iron deficiency without anemia (IDWA) is the stage before — when ferritin is depleted but hemoglobin hasn't fallen yet. ASH 2023 and AAFP both flag IDWA as frequently missed.
What's the right ferritin target?
Lab reference ranges typically start at 15 ng/mL — set decades ago to detect frank iron deficiency anemia, not symptomatic deficiency. Modern guidelines: ferritin below 30 is treatment-warranted; below 50 often impairs thyroid and cognitive function; optimal target for active women is 50-100 ng/mL.
What is TSAT and why does it matter?
Transferrin saturation (TSAT) measures how much iron is actually being delivered to cells. Below 20% indicates poor delivery even when ferritin is in lab-normal range.
How does inflammation affect ferritin?
Ferritin is also an acute-phase reactant — it rises with inflammation independent of iron status. Lipa interprets ferritin alongside hs-CRP and flags inflammation-confounded cases.
Why do hypothyroid women often have iron deficiency?
Bidirectional. Iron is a cofactor for thyroid peroxidase. Hypothyroidism affects menstrual blood loss. Women with Hashimoto's have higher rates of iron deficiency than the general population.
