The pattern we see every day

"My doctor said everything looks fine. So why do I feel like this?"

Hashimoto's is the most common autoimmune disease on the planet. Up to 10% of women carry it. Yet most patients are managed with a single number: TSH. When TSH is "in range," the conversation usually ends.

It shouldn't.

A normal TSH with raging antibodies, low free T3, and depleted ferritin is not a healthy thyroid — it's a thyroid that has not yet failed enough for TSH to flag it.

The clinical research is clear: 5-10% of treated Hashimoto's patients remain symptomatic despite biochemical "euthyroidism" because their interpretation stopped at TSH. The story is in the rest of the panel — the antibodies still attacking, the T4 not converting to T3, the ferritin or vitamin D quietly undermining the whole system.

What Lipa catches

The patterns most thyroid care misses.

These are the interpretations that turn a "your labs are fine" appointment into "here's exactly what to fix." Lipa's pattern engine flags every one of them on every panel.

Pattern · 1

The poor-converter phenotype

Normal TSH and free T4, but low free T3. About 80% of T3 comes from peripheral conversion — and 5-10% of patients can't convert efficiently. Selenium, iron, cortisol, and inflammation all blunt the deiodinase enzymes that do the job. Lipa flags this pattern on every panel where it appears.

Pattern · 2

Functional iron deficiency

Most labs flag ferritin below 15 ng/mL. But thyroid peroxidase requires iron, and ferritin below 50-70 ng/mL impairs thyroid hormone synthesis even when CBC looks normal. We use Hashimoto-specific targets, not lab cutoffs.

Pattern · 3

Vitamin D below the autoimmune threshold

Lab cutoffs say "deficient below 20." Hashimoto research says antibodies decline with vitamin D above 40-60 ng/mL. We flag everything below the autoimmune threshold and give you the supplementation protocol with K2 co-factor included.

Pattern · 4

Antibody trajectory, not just value

If you've uploaded multiple panels, Lipa shows your TPO/TgAb trend over time. Are interventions actually slowing the autoimmune attack? The slope matters more than the snapshot.

Pattern · 5

The nutrient cluster

Hashimoto's clusters with B12 deficiency, celiac disease, low iron, and adrenal stress. We screen for all of them on every panel — patterns most endocrinologists don't have time to look for.

Pattern · 6

Drug interaction warnings

Levothyroxine absorption is wrecked by iron, calcium, coffee, PPIs, and GLP-1 agonists if timing is wrong. Lipa flags interactions in your action plan with explicit timing recommendations — what to take when, and what to keep apart.

Common questions.

Why is my TSH normal but I still feel terrible?
TSH is one number among many. A "normal" TSH doesn't tell you whether your antibodies are still attacking, whether T4 is converting to T3 efficiently, or whether iron, vitamin D, B12, or selenium deficiencies are blunting thyroid function. Approximately 5-10% of treated Hashimoto's patients remain symptomatic with "normal" labs because their interpretation stopped at TSH. Lipa interprets the full panel.
What's the complete Hashimoto's blood panel?
TSH, free T4, free T3, TPO antibodies, thyroglobulin antibodies (TgAb), reverse T3, vitamin D (25-OH), ferritin, serum iron, B12, selenium, and ideally hs-CRP and fasting glucose. Most doctors run only TSH and free T4. Lipa interprets all of these together — against Hashimoto-specific optimal ranges, not just lab reference ranges.
What is the "poor T4-to-T3 converter" phenotype?
About 80% of T3 (the active thyroid hormone) is produced by converting T4 to T3 in peripheral tissues. Some Hashimoto's patients have normal TSH and free T4 but low free T3 — meaning the body isn't converting efficiently. This explains why 5-10% of patients on T4-only levothyroxine remain symptomatic despite "normal" labs. Conversion is impaired by selenium deficiency, iron deficiency, chronic stress, inflammation, and certain medications. All measurable, all addressable.
How quickly can I upload and see results?
Upload a PDF or photo. Lipa extracts every marker and starts analysis in 30 seconds. Marker-level status appears within minutes; the full action plan completes in 5-15 minutes depending on panel size. You'll get an email when it's ready.
Is Lipa medical advice?
No. Lipa is educational research-grounded interpretation, not medical diagnosis or treatment. Every recommendation is grounded in peer-reviewed research, but you should always discuss changes — especially around levothyroxine dosing or supplement interactions — with your healthcare provider. We make it easier to walk into your appointment armed with the right questions.
What if my country uses different units (e.g. SI vs US)?
Lipa auto-detects units (mIU/L vs μIU/mL, ng/mL vs nmol/L, mg/dL vs mmol/L) and converts where needed. Polish lab, German lab, UK NHS, US Quest, anywhere — same interpretation engine.
