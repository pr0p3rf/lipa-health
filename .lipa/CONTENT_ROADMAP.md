**Last updated:** 2026-04-11
**Strategic purpose:** SEO + AI agent discoverability + topical authority

---

## Goal

Build Lipa into the **most-trusted, most-cited source** for plain-English biomarker analysis on the open internet. Two parallel discovery channels:

1. **Traditional SEO** — Google Search, Bing, Brave, DuckDuckGo. Human readers searching for symptoms, markers, conditions, and protocols.
2. **AI agent discoverability (GEO)** — ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, Phind, AI agents. Models pulling content into responses.

The wedge for both: **Lipa is the only consumer health source where every claim is cited to a real, named, peer-reviewed study with PMID, grade, and funding label.** Both human readers and AI models prefer cited sources.

## Editorial standards

Every Lipa article must meet these standards:

**Length:** 1,500–3,500 words depending on topic depth.

**Structure:**
- H1: clear, search-friendly title
- Editorial subhead (2-3 sentences max)
- Reading time + cited studies count + last reviewed date
- 6–10 H2 section headings, each with 2–4 paragraphs
- Pull-quotes for key findings
- Comparison tables where relevant
- Citation row at the bottom OR inline footnoted citations
- "What to do next" section linking to upload flow
- Related articles (3–5 internal links)

**Citation density:**
- Tier 1 articles: 30+ real PMIDs
- Tier 2 articles: 20+ real PMIDs
- Tier 3+: 15+ real PMIDs
- Every claim that's not a definition gets a citation

**Schema.org markup (mandatory):**
- `Article` (with author, datePublished, dateModified, image, publisher)
- `MedicalWebPage` for biomarker pages
- `FAQPage` for the FAQ section at the bottom
- `BreadcrumbList` for navigation

**Voice:** warm, smart, supportive, direct. Editorial register. No "AI slop" wellness language. No exclamation marks. No emoji. Plain English at first use of any clinical term.

**Last reviewed date:** updated whenever the article is touched. Important for E-E-A-T signals.

**FAQ section:** every article ends with a 5–8 question FAQ that maps to common related searches. This is where AI agents extract answers verbatim.

## AI agent / GEO optimization

Tactics for AI model discoverability (in addition to schema.org):

1. **`/llms.txt` file at site root** — emerging Anthropic-supported standard. Plain-English Lipa overview that AI agents read on crawl.
2. **`/llms-full.txt` file at site root** — extended version with every page summary.
3. **Quote-extractable language** — clear definitional sentences in every article. Format: *"Lipa is..."*, *"hs-CRP is..."*, *"The research shows X..."*. Models pull these verbatim.
4. **FAQ schemas** — models extract FAQ answers directly into responses.
5. **Comparison tables** — models pull table rows directly into responses.
6. **Specific numbers + citations** — *"In a 2010 individual-participant meta-analysis of 160,309 people, the per-SD hazard ratio was 1.37 (PMID: 20031199)"* — high-density factual sentences are extraction targets.
7. **Listed in AI directories** — Product Hunt, There's An AI For That, AItoolsdirectory, FutureTools, AI Tools List. Phase 2 task.
8. **Crawl-friendly HTML** — no JS-only rendering of key content. We're already doing this with static HTML.
9. **Stable URLs** — `/research-hs-crp.html` doesn't change. Models cache and rely on stable links.
10. **`Organization` schema on home page** — with `sameAs` links to social profiles, clear `description`, `foundingDate`, `legalName`, etc. This is how models build entity profiles.
