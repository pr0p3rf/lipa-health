## What Lipa is — the five pillars

Lipa isn't a "research-backed analysis tool." It's a health product that delivers five distinct things, every time, on every plan. This is the agreed positioning locked on 2026-04-11 and should be used consistently across every surface.

### 1. Understanding
A plain-English explanation of every biomarker in your test — what it means for you, what the research actually shows, what patterns you're sitting in. Not a red/green chart. Not a PDF with jargon. Understanding.

### 2. A personalized action plan
Across six domains — **nutrition, supplementation, sleep, movement, environment, lifestyle** — with every recommendation tied to research. Not a generic wellness plan. Not an influencer's supplement stack. A plan built from your specific biology and the peer-reviewed literature on your specific markers.

### 3. A vault of your biology over time
Every test, permanent. Year-over-year trending. Cross-marker pattern detection that only becomes visible when you look at multiple tests together. Your biology as a continuous record instead of a series of disconnected PDFs.

### 4. Peer-reviewed calculations applied to your numbers
Not proprietary "Lipa scores." Real, named, published methodologies: SCORE2, HOMA-IR, TyG Index, Castelli Risk Index, Atherogenic Index of Plasma, FIB-4, AST/ALT Ratio, KDM Bio-Age, CKD-EPI eGFR, NLR, TG/HDL Ratio, fT3/fT4 ratio, and more. Clinical-grade risk estimation for longevity, cardiovascular, metabolic, liver, and kidney health. **16+ calculations today, roadmap to 20+.**

### 5. A living research engine working for you
The only "research" pillar. Lipa's engine ingests new studies every day from PubMed, Europe PMC, Cochrane, ClinicalTrials.gov, bioRxiv, medRxiv, and major clinical guideline bodies (ESC, ADA, EASL, WHO, NICE, USPSTF). Every paper is categorized, funding-tagged, evidence-graded, and — when something lands that's relevant to a specific member's biology — matched and delivered as a personalized insight. This is "Living Research™" in its literal form.

## The wedge — what makes Lipa different

**Bring Your Own Test (BYOT) + research-grade depth.** No competitor combines both.

- **US upmarket players** (Function Health $365/yr, Superpower $199, InsideTracker $489+, Mito $349, Lifeforce $550) force you into their lab network. BYOT is absent or thin.
- **US BYOT players** (Kantesti, VitaDash, BloodGPT, Healthmatters, Carrot Care, SmarterBlood) are LLM wrappers with no curated corpus, no citations, no grading, no funding transparency.
- **European test providers** (Thriva, Medichecks, Randox, Forth, Lola, Numan, LetsGetChecked) are test providers — not analysis platforms. They give you PDFs, not understanding.
- **Lucis** is the closest upmarket EU competitor. Does action plans. Doesn't cite studies. Doesn't track funding. Doesn't do BYOT.

Lipa sits in the empty quadrant: BYOT + deep, cited analysis + personalized action plan + vault + peer-reviewed risk calculations + living research engine.

**Key differentiator against every other "AI health" product:** every insight is linked to a real, named, peer-reviewed study you can click through and read. No hallucinated citations. No "proprietary AI score." Nothing we can't cite.

Lipa is built for people who already take their health seriously and have run into the ceiling of thin analysis and generic advice.

**Primary segments:**
- **TRT / hormone optimization** — protocols touch 20+ markers, doctor watches a handful
- **Peptide research** — signal/noise problem, Reddit is unreliable, research exists

- **Perimenopause / menopause** — shifting hormones, under-supported in routine care
- **Chronic condition management** — Hashimoto's, T2D, PCOS, autoimmune — need trending and pattern detection

These audiences test 4-6x per year and pay for health. They are the retention base and the word-of-mouth engine.

## Key risks & mitigations

### 1. Funding-extraction and insight quality
**Risk:** Personalized research insights could deliver low-quality or misleading content at scale.
**Mitigation:** Launch Grade A+ only for 90 days. Human spot-check weekly. Conservative rate limits.

### 2. No clinical advisor today
**Risk:** Can't claim "reviewed by clinical advisory team" without a real clinician on the page.
**Mitigation:** Use safe framing (*"built on peer-reviewed clinical methodologies"*). Onboard 1-2 EU clinicians within 30 days (€5-15K/yr each). Upgrade to full "clinical advisory team" language once named.

### 3. Competitive response
**Risk:** Lucis or Function could clone the BYOT + citation angle.
**Mitigation:** Category leadership is sticky. Curated per-biomarker library compounds. Personalized insights pipeline is a full system, not a weekend clone. GDPR + EU residency is a real moat.

### 4. "Why not just ChatGPT?"
**Risk:** Users could paste their test into a chatbot.
**Mitigation:** Curated corpus, no hallucinated citations, 24h research refresh, funding tracking, vault, personalized matching. General LLMs have none of these. Explicitly addressed on the methodology page.

### 5. Ad platform health-claim friction
**Risk:** Meta/Google screen health claims hard. Some of our copy could trip filters.
**Mitigation:** Educational framing throughout. No disease/treatment claims. Legal review of all ad copy before launch.

### 6. Pharma-funded research skepticism
**Mitigation:** Built funding transparency into methodology and the research engine. Every study tagged with funding source. Independent research weighted higher. We don't attack pharma, we show who paid.
