The pattern we see every day

"My doctor said the labs were fine. So I must just be anxious."

You had COVID — months ago, maybe a year, maybe two. The acute infection passed. But you didn't come back. Fatigue that crushes you after activity. Brain fog that scares you. Heart racing when you stand up. Sleep that doesn't restore. You go to your doctor armed with a list. They run a basic panel. Everything looks "in range."

The conversation pivots to depression, anxiety, deconditioning. They suggest you try yoga. You leave gaslit.

Long COVID doesn't show up as one number out of range. It shows up as five subtle clusters that need to be read together. Inflammation that doesn't match an active infection. HPA axis that's hypofunctional, not over-activated. T3 conversion blocked by cytokines. Vitamins depleted. Autonomic markers off. Each individually looks "fine." Together they form a recognizable pattern.

Lipa reads the pattern. We can't fix what doesn't have an approved treatment. But we can show you it's not in your head — and surface the addressable parts.

Lipa's long-COVID interpretation
Five-cluster long-COVID pattern, textbook. Persistent inflammation (hs-CRP 3.4 + fibrinogen 410) that's not from acute infection — your CBC says no active infection. HPA axis hypofunction (AM cortisol 8.2 + DHEA-S 68 floor) — your stress system is exhausted, not over-activated, which is why "manage your stress" doesn't help and may make things worse. Cytokine-blocked T3 conversion (TSH 1.8 normal, fT4 mid, but fT3 at the floor) — euthyroid sick syndrome / non-thyroidal illness syndrome. You feel hypothyroid because you functionally are, but TSH-only screening dismisses you. Severe vitamin D deficiency (19 ng/mL) — independently impairs immune recovery and amplifies fatigue. Suboptimal iron and B12 add to the load. Plan: vitamin D3 10,000 IU/day for 12 weeks then 5000 IU + K2 MK-7, iron bisglycinate 25mg + vitamin C alternate-day mornings, methylcobalamin B12 1000mcg sublingual daily, anti-inflammatory diet (Mediterranean + omega-3 3g daily), pacing protocol (no graded exercise — that crashed you), stress modulation through nervous-system tools (yoga nidra, slow nasal breathing) NOT through "exercise more" advice, sleep prioritization with magnesium glycinate 400mg pre-bed. Consider asking your doctor about low-dose naltrexone (LDN) — research evidence is emerging for long COVID. Retest at 12 weeks; expect inflammation and vitamin D to move first, fT3 and cortisol to lag.

What Lipa catches

The five-cluster pattern most clinicians don't read.

Cluster · 1

Persistent inflammation without infection

hs-CRP >2 + fibrinogen upper-range + normal CBC = chronic inflammation independent of acute infection. Long COVID research consistently shows this signature.

Cluster · 2

HPA axis hypofunction

Low AM cortisol + low DHEA-S = HPA exhaustion, NOT chronic-stress activation. The treatment direction is opposite. Lipa flags this distinction.

Cluster · 3

Cytokine-blocked T3 conversion

Normal TSH and fT4 with low fT3 = euthyroid sick syndrome. You feel hypothyroid because you functionally are, but TSH-only screening dismisses you.

Cluster · 4

Micronutrient depletion stacking

Vitamin D, ferritin, B12 frequently co-depleted in long COVID. Each individually looks "low-normal." Together they amplify fatigue and immune dysfunction.

Cluster · 5

Autonomic / orthostatic markers

POTS-like symptoms often have weak lab signals individually. Lipa flags NT-proBNP, troponin, and electrolyte patterns when symptoms suggest dysautonomia.

Approach

Pacing > graded exercise

The single most important intervention reversal: graded exercise therapy crashed many long COVID patients. Pacing (matching activity to envelope) is the safer protocol. Lipa flags this in every long-COVID action plan.

Common questions.

Is there a diagnostic blood test for long COVID?
Not yet, but research is closing in. A 2025 study published in Brain, Behavior, and Immunity found a 3-marker combination (C5a, TGFβ1, Gliomedin) with 94% sensitivity for distinguishing long COVID from controls. Nature 2023 (Iwasaki lab) identified five immune subtypes. None routine yet — but the patterns visible on standard bloodwork are well-documented.
What patterns does Lipa look for?
Five clusters: (1) inflammation — hs-CRP, fibrinogen, IL-6 if available; (2) HPA dysfunction — low AM cortisol, low DHEA-S; (3) thyroid conversion — normal TSH but low fT3; (4) micronutrient depletion — vitamin D, ferritin, B12; (5) autonomic markers when POTS-like.
Why is fT3 important in post-viral syndromes?
"Non-thyroidal illness syndrome" (euthyroid sick / low-T3 syndrome) is well-described. Cytokines impair the deiodinase enzymes that convert T4 to T3. TSH and fT4 stay normal; fT3 drops. Lipa flags low-normal or below-range fT3 with normal TSH.
What about HPA axis dysfunction?
Low AM cortisol with low DHEA-S is one of the most consistent findings in long COVID. The HPA axis appears hypofunctional rather than over-activated. AM cortisol <10 with DHEA-S in the bottom quartile is a Lipa flag. Treatment direction is opposite to the chronic-stress assumption.
Can long COVID be reversed?
Variable. A subset of patients improve significantly with structured intervention (paced activity, anti-inflammatory diet, micronutrient repletion, sleep optimization, stress modulation). Others have persistent symptoms. Lipa is not curative — we read the bloodwork and identify the addressable patterns.
