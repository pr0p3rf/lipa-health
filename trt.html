You started TRT to fix energy, libido, recovery. Three months in, the bloodwork comes back. Total T is up. Your prescriber says "looks great." But you don't feel great — sleep is shallow, mood is flat, gym performance is plateauing.

Common questions.

Why isn't total testosterone enough?
Total testosterone measures bound + unbound. Most testosterone is bound to SHBG and inactive — only free testosterone interacts with receptors. A man with total T 1100 ng/dL and SHBG 16 has lower bioavailable testosterone than one with total T 700 and SHBG 35. Lipa interprets free T and SHBG together — the only way to know what's actually circulating.
Why does sensitive estradiol matter on TRT?
Standard estradiol assays are calibrated for women and lose accuracy below 50 pg/mL — the range most TRT users live in. Sensitive E2 (LC-MS/MS) is accurate down to single digits, which is the only way to dial in aromatase inhibitor dosing or determine if symptoms are from low E2 vs high E2.
When should I be worried about hematocrit?
The conventional cutoff for therapeutic phlebotomy is hematocrit 54%. Lipa flags hematocrit ≥51% as needing attention — hydration check, dose review, donation timing — before it becomes a clinical issue. Above 54% is doctor-call territory.
Does TRT raise PSA?
TRT can produce a small initial PSA bump in the first 3-6 months as testosterone levels normalize, then stabilize. The signal we watch for is rate of change — PSA velocity above 0.4 ng/mL per year, or absolute PSA above age-appropriate cutoffs. Lipa tracks PSA over time when you upload multiple panels.
Can Lipa interpret post-cycle / SARM recovery panels?
Yes. Most clinicians won't engage with PCT protocols, but the bloodwork patterns are well-characterized. Lipa interprets recovery panels: HPTA function, lipid impact, liver markers, prolactin. Educational only — Lipa is not a prescriber.
