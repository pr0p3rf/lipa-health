How it works

From your upload
to cited insight.

Six deliberate steps. Every insight you see is grounded in real peer-reviewed studies — not a model's best guess.

01

Read the research

Our engine continuously ingests new studies from the world's major medical databases and guideline bodies. We keep only research that meets a quality bar: human studies, recent enough to be relevant, published in peer-reviewed venues, and from study types that actually inform clinical decisions — randomized trials, systematic reviews, large cohorts, and official guidelines. 80,000+ curated studies and growing.

02

Understand your test

When you upload a blood test in any format — PDF, photo, scan — we identify every biomarker, extract your value, and normalize it against the standard units used in research. We recognize tests from European, UK, and North American labs, in English, Polish, Dutch, Spanish, and German.

03

Match you to the research

For each of your markers, we find the studies in our library that are most relevant to your specific value range, weighted by evidence quality and how recent the research is. Higher-grade studies — systematic reviews, major guidelines, large trials — are prioritized above smaller or older papers. Your optimal ranges are adjusted by age and sex using NHANES population data (300K+ profiles) and published clinical guidelines.

04

Detect cross-marker patterns

Individual markers tell part of the story. Lipa detects 6 clinical cross-marker patterns — combinations of markers that, together, tell you something neither marker reveals alone. Metabolic syndrome signatures, thyroid conversion issues, inflammatory cascades, iron metabolism patterns, cardiovascular risk clusters, and hormonal axis patterns are identified automatically across your full panel.

05

Calculate your risk and biological age

We run 16+ peer-reviewed risk calculations on your panel — SCORE2, HOMA-IR, FIB-4, eGFR, AIP, and more — each from a named, published methodology. We also compute your biological age using an ensemble of KDM (Klemera-Doubal) and PhenoAge algorithms, and compare your results to population percentiles by age and sex.

06

Explain and cite

We compose an analysis for each marker that is grounded exclusively in the studies we retrieved — no guesses, no invented references, no hallucinated PMIDs. Every sentence ties back to a specific paper. You can click any citation and land on the real study on PubMed.

Evidence grading

Not all studies
carry the same weight.

A guideline from the European Society of Cardiology isn't the same as a small preliminary study. We grade every citation so you can see at a glance how strong the evidence is — and we prioritize higher-graded research when studies disagree.

Grade A+
Extensive
Systematic reviews and meta-analyses of randomized controlled trials. Cochrane reviews, published guidelines from major bodies (ESC, ADA, WHO, Endocrine Society).
e.g. SCORE2 working group · Eur Heart J 2021
Grade A
High
Individual well-designed randomized controlled trials, large prospective cohort studies with long follow-up, robust replication.
e.g. Emerging Risk Factors Collaboration · Lancet
Grade B
Moderate
Smaller RCTs, cross-sectional studies, case-control studies. Methodologically sound but limited by sample size or duration.
e.g. NHANES cohort analysis · AJCN
Grade C
Emerging
Preliminary studies, animal models with clear human relevance, small observational series. Cited only when higher-grade evidence is sparse and labeled clearly.
e.g. early mechanistic papers
Funding transparency

Who paid for the research
matters.

A randomized trial funded by the NIH reads differently than a manufacturer-funded single-site study. Both can be valid research. But knowing which is which matters — and most platforms don't show you.

Pharma-funded research isn't inherently wrong. Some of the best medicine we have came from industry-sponsored trials, and rigorous pharma studies are often larger and better-monitored than independent ones. But knowing who paid for a piece of research is context you deserve.

Every paper in Lipa's engine is tagged with its funding source. Government grants, academic departments, non-profit foundations, pharma, device makers, food industry, mixed, or undeclared — whatever the source, it's extracted at ingestion and visible on every insight that cites it.

Independently-funded research gets a heavier weighting in our grading than industry-sponsored work. Studies with undeclared funding get a significant penalty. When Lipa surfaces an insight to you, you see the funding label — so you can judge for yourself, instead of trusting a black-box score.

We don't attack pharma research. We just don't hide it.

hs-CRP
Inflammatory · cardiovascular
Borderline
2.8 mg/L
Range: 0.0 – 3.0
What the research shows

Values in the 1.0–3.0 mg/L range are associated with moderate cardiovascular risk in multiple large prospective cohort studies. The Emerging Risk Factors Collaboration (2010) analyzed 160,309 participants and found a per-SD hazard ratio of 1.37 (95% CI 1.27–1.48) for coronary heart disease, independent of traditional risk factors. More recent meta-analyses have confirmed the dose-response relationship and shown that intervention to lower hs-CRP (statins, lifestyle) reduces event rates in primary prevention.

SCORE2
Cardiovascular · 10-yr risk
Derived from the 2021 European Society of Cardiology SCORE2 working group analysis of 677,684 participants across 45 cohorts. Estimates 10-year fatal and non-fatal cardiovascular event risk.
SCORE2 working group · Eur Heart J 2021;42(25):2439–2454
PMID: 34120177
HOMA-IR
Metabolic · insulin resistance
Homeostatic Model Assessment of Insulin Resistance. Calculated from fasting glucose and fasting insulin. The standard surrogate measure of insulin resistance in non-diabetic populations.
Matthews DR et al. · Diabetologia 1985;28(7):412–419
PMID: 3899825
TyG Index
Metabolic · triglyceride-glucose
Triglyceride-glucose index, a validated surrogate for insulin resistance that requires only a standard lipid panel and fasting glucose. Useful where fasting insulin isn't available.
Simental-Mendia LE et al. · Metab Syndr Relat Disord 2008;6(4):299–304
PMID: 19067533
FIB-4
Liver · fibrosis
Non-invasive index for advanced liver fibrosis. Calculated from age, AST, ALT, and platelet count. Originally developed for HCV-HIV coinfection, now validated broadly across NAFLD and general populations.
Sterling RK et al. · Hepatology 2006;43(6):1317–1325
PMID: 16729309
KDM Bio-Age
Longevity · biological age
Klemera-Doubal method for biological age estimation. Uses a simplified 8-biomarker panel (albumin, creatinine, glucose, CRP, MCV, RDW, alkaline phosphatase, WBC) to estimate age deviation from chronological age. Part of our ensemble bio-age calculation alongside PhenoAge.
Klemera P, Doubal S · Mech Ageing Dev 2006;127(3):240–248
PMID: 16318865
PhenoAge
Longevity · biological age
Levine's PhenoAge biological age algorithm, trained on mortality data from NHANES III. Uses 9 clinical chemistry markers plus chronological age to estimate phenotypic age. Part of our ensemble bio-age calculation alongside KDM.
Levine ME et al. · Aging 2018;10(4):573–591
PMID: 29676998
eGFR (CKD-EPI 2021)
Kidney · glomerular filtration
The race-free 2021 CKD-EPI equation for estimated glomerular filtration rate. The current standard for kidney function assessment, removing race as a coefficient.
Inker LA et al. · N Engl J Med 2021;385(19):1737–1749
PMID: 34554658
Castelli Risk Index
Cardiovascular · TC/HDL
Ratio of total cholesterol to HDL cholesterol. One of the original composite lipid risk markers, still used as a simple adjunct to more sophisticated models.
Castelli WP · Am J Med 1986;80(2A):23–32
PMID: 3456638
Atherogenic Index of Plasma
Cardiovascular · lipoprotein size
log10(TG/HDL-C), a validated marker of atherogenic lipoprotein particle size. Studied extensively as an adjunct to standard lipid panels for cardiovascular risk stratification.
Dobiasova M, Frohlich J · Clin Biochem 2001;34(7):583–588
PMID: 11738396

Honest limitations

What Lipa is not.

Transparency cuts both ways. Here's what we don't do, what we can't do, and where our analysis has limits you should know about.

— NOT —
A medical diagnosis.
Lipa is educational content. Every analysis is framed as "what the research shows" — not "what is wrong with you." Nothing on this platform replaces consultation with a licensed healthcare provider.
— NOT —
A regulated medical device.
Lipa is not CE-marked or FDA-cleared as a medical device. We make no claims to treat, diagnose, cure, or prevent any disease. Our outputs are research interpretation, not clinical decisions.
— LIMIT —
Bio-age is an estimate.
Biological age from blood biomarkers is a research calculation with wide confidence intervals. Different methods (KDM, PhenoAge, DunedinPACE) produce different numbers. We show an ensemble estimate using both KDM and PhenoAge, and label it clearly as illustrative.
— LIMIT —
Research is ongoing.
Evidence evolves. A study cited today may be superseded next year. Our continuous corpus refresh is designed to surface new evidence quickly, but at any moment you are reading a snapshot.
— LIMIT —
Individual variation is real.
Population-level studies inform what markers mean on average. Your individual biology, genetics, and lifestyle may deviate from those averages. We show ranges and context, not definitive personal predictions.
— BY DESIGN —
We don't recommend drugs.
Lipa never recommends prescription medications or dosages. Where research discusses therapeutic interventions, we present the evidence neutrally and direct you to discuss options with your clinician.
