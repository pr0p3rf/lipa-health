Popular peptides — what to monitor.

BPC-157
Body Protective Compound · Pentadecapeptide BPC 157

Gastric peptide derivative used for tendon, ligament, and gut healing. The most-used peptide in the recovery space. FDA reclassified for compounding restrictions in 2023.

Markers to track
  • hs-CRP (anti-inflammatory response)
  • Fibrinogen (coagulation status)
  • ALT, AST, GGT (liver, especially with stacks)
  • eGFR, creatinine (kidney load)
  • CK if rehab from injury
Expected response (8-12 weeks)
  • hs-CRP trending down 20-40%
  • Liver markers stable
  • Symptom improvement preceding lab signal

TB-500
Thymosin Beta-4 fragment

Thymosin-derived peptide used for tendon, muscle, and wound healing. Often stacked with BPC-157. Longer half-life than BPC; typically dosed weekly.

Markers to track
  • hs-CRP, ESR (systemic inflammation)
  • CK (muscle damage / recovery)
  • WBC differential (immune modulation)
  • Liver enzymes
Stacking note
  • BPC-157 + TB-500 is the most common healing stack
  • Combined liver load worth watching

CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin
GHRH analogue + GHRP — the GH secretagogue stack

The most-used growth hormone stack outside of TRT clinics. CJC-1295 extends GH pulses; ipamorelin amplifies them. Dosed evenings, subcutaneous.

Markers to track
  • IGF-1 (the headline)
  • Fasting glucose + insulin (GH-induced IR)
  • HbA1c (8-12 week trajectory)
  • Prolactin (lower vs MK-677, but watch)
  • Cortisol (if symptoms suggest)
  • TSH, free T3 (GH affects deiodinase)
Target IGF-1
  • Upper third of age-appropriate reference
  • Above that = insulin resistance, fluid retention, joint pain risk

MK-677 (Ibutamoren)
Oral ghrelin mimetic · long-acting GH secretagogue

Oral, once-daily. Drives strong GH/IGF-1 release. The most powerful and the most side-effect-prone of the GH-secretagogue family. Two well-documented effects: insulin resistance and elevated prolactin.

Markers to track
  • IGF-1 (rises significantly — often 2x baseline)
  • Fasting glucose + insulin (will rise)
  • HbA1c (often +0.2-0.4% over 8-12 weeks)
  • Prolactin (often 2-4x baseline)
  • HDL (may suppress)
  • Cortisol
Discontinue or dose-reduce if
  • A1c crosses 5.7%
  • Prolactin >25 ng/mL with symptoms
  • Edema or carpal tunnel

GHK-Cu
Copper peptide · Glycyl-L-histidyl-L-lysine + Cu²⁺

Copper-binding tripeptide. Skin regeneration, wound healing, hair growth signals. Topical or subcutaneous. Generally well-tolerated; one of the safest peptides.

Markers to track
  • Serum copper (rare but worth a baseline)
  • Ceruloplasmin (copper transport)
  • hs-CRP, fibrinogen (anti-inflammatory effect)
  • Ferritin (iron-copper interaction)
Note
  • Direct bloodwork impact is modest
  • Skin/hair response is observed clinically rather than via labs

AOD-9604
Anti-Obesity Drug 9604 · GH-fragment 176-191

Synthetic fragment of the GH chain. Originally developed for obesity. Selectively activates lipolysis without the GH/IGF-1 elevation. Useful for body composition without insulin resistance trade-offs.

Markers to track
  • Fasting glucose + insulin (should NOT rise)
  • Free fatty acids (rise during dosing)
  • Lipid panel + ApoB
  • IGF-1 (should stay flat — distinguishes from GH stacks)

Selank & Semax
Russian-developed nootropic peptides · ACTH-derived

Intranasal cognitive peptides. Selank for anxiolytic effect, Semax for focus and BDNF support. Russian regulatory approval; OTC in some jurisdictions.

  • Cortisol AM (HPA axis modulation)
  • DHEA-S
  • Prolactin (rare baseline)
Note

Common questions.

What bloodwork should I track on peptides?
Depends on the peptide. GH secretagogues (CJC, ipamorelin, sermorelin, MK-677) require IGF-1, fasting glucose, fasting insulin, prolactin, cortisol, TSH. Healing peptides (BPC-157, TB-500) require hs-CRP, fibrinogen, ALT/AST/GGT, eGFR. Cognitive peptides (Selank, Semax, DSIP) have minimal direct lab impact but affect HPA axis.
Why is IGF-1 important on GH-secretagogue peptides?
CJC, ipamorelin, sermorelin, and MK-677 stimulate endogenous GH, which converts to IGF-1 in the liver. IGF-1 is the integrated readout. Target is the upper third of age-appropriate reference. Above that = insulin resistance, fluid retention, joint pain risk. Below that = underdosed or quality suspect.
Why does MK-677 raise glucose and prolactin?
MK-677 is an oral ghrelin mimetic. Two well-documented effects: GH-induced insulin resistance (A1c often +0.2-0.4% over 12 weeks) and elevated prolactin from ghrelin receptor cross-activity. Lipa flags both and adjusts the action plan if A1c crosses 5.7%.
Are BPC-157 and TB-500 detectable on bloodwork?
Not directly — no commercial assay. The response is detectable: hs-CRP and fibrinogen typically decline. Lipa tracks the trajectory across panels.
What's the regulatory status?
Varies by jurisdiction. FDA reclassified BPC-157 in 2023. Many peptides sold as "research chemicals." Lipa is educational interpretation — we read your bloodwork. We don't sell, prescribe, or advise on legality.
